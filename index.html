About Me

I am Vishal Kumar Singh, an undergraduate student of the department of mining engineering. I am from Gorakhpur, Uttar Pradesh. This website is not just made to bring me closer to you but also to change KGP for the better. I am a person who loves interacting with people, learning from their experiences and trying to incorporate those learnings in my life.     Students' Alumni Cell

    11 hours ago via Twitter

    Students’ Alumni Cell, IIT Kharagpur is a voluntary student body working under the aegis of the Dean of Alumni Affairs. The main purpose of Students’ Alumni Cell is to keep the alumni connected to the institute so that they can comfortably give back to the institute. The giving back need not just be in a monetary way but also in the form of guidance to the existing students. I have been a part of Students’ Alumni Cell for two years now, first as student member and then as the General Secretary. As a student member I was part of the executive committee which conducted the 14th Annual Alumni Meet along with many other initiatives.
    As the General Secretary I was entrusted with the responsibility of developing and expanding the cell along with the team of my 10 other coordinators. I have tried my best to fulfil my responsibility to the best of my capability. Under my leadership the 15th Annual Alumni Meet reached new heights. It had the highest number of alumni attendees this year since its inception 15 years ago. I also recognized a very prevalent issue in the campus regarding the final year students who face difficulties in the CDC placement. So we thought of connecting the most recent alumni i.e. the last year’s graduating batch to the final year students. It proved to be really effective with overwhelming positive response.
    Another major initiative conceptualized by me and materialized by me and my second year team was the affinity program. It is a program aimed at our huge alumni base and the existing students. Under this program there will be exclusive benefits such as discounts and cashbacks to the KGP student and alumni community. This program, though in its infant stages, has huge potential to develop in the future. Not only does it maintain healthy corporate relations with a huge base of companies but also puts us in the very elite group educational institutes to have such a program.

    Ernst & Young

    Ernst and Young or EY, is a renowned professional services company. It is a multinational company headquartered in London. I got the wonderful opportunity to do an internship during May 2017. I obtained a lot of corporate experience that I expected, from EY. I designed information memorandum which is a detailed report and guidelines for investors under the national project of “Make in India”. Using the sharp analytical mind that I developed during my stay in KGP, I studied investment queries from the portal of Ministry of Commerce & Industry to enlist and filter investors’ details. In addition to this I also assisted the advisory team in drafting the final report of potential investment opportunities in “Make in India” project.

    Rankethon

    Rankethon is an institution which trains students in technical fields, give them the required mindset for the startup culture and finally give them incubation as well as mentorship for them to enter the corporate world with their idea. The very idea of this startup intrigued me in my first year. As the city head I organized Summer Camp Classes for Web and App Development for the very first time for schools in Kanpur and Malda. This ignited the young minds of the schools involved and they developed a technical temperament. In my second year I extensively planned a tech-integration program for classes 8 to 12 which led to the establishment of Digital Learning System and Tech Laboratories. This enabled the students of those schools to participate in Techkriti, the tech-fest of IIT-BHU, making them the first and only student team to ever participate in Techkriti. Currently Rankethon is growing at a fast rate due to the tie up with “100 open startups” enabling it to help other tech startups to establish their place in the corporate world.
